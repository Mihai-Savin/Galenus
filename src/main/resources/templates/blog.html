STREP THROAT

Strep throat is a contagious disease, which means you catch it from another person. It is caused by infection with bacteria called group A streptococci (pronounced: strep-toe-KAH-kye) bacteria, and it's very common among teens. In fact, strep bacteria cause almost a third of all sore throats. The symptoms of strep throat include sore throat, fever, and stomach pain, along with red and swollen tonsils.
